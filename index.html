London's Sites by Borough
London is a city brimming with history, culture, and natural charm. Each borough has its own tourism profile — such as Big Ben in Westminster or cultural museums in Kensington and Chelsea — reflecting the city's rich and varied character.

Conclusion

This project aims to respond to the post-pandemic recovery of the UK tourism sector by developing an interactive travel planning platform for London, in light of increasing demand for urban mobility and cultural engagement. The platform includes a personalised route planner that generates customised itineraries based on spatial-temporal constraints and individual points of interest. It also visualises key tourism trends—such as visitor numbers, spending levels, and average stay durations—and provides spatial analysis of attraction density across the city. By integrating behavioural insight with geospatial data, the platform reflects the complex relationships between movement, space, and experience. Situated within the conceptual framework of "City Networks", these travel trajectories are interpreted as dynamic linkages between people, places, and digital infrastructures. While the current version presents a functional prototype, it operates on static input data and limited contextual responsiveness. Future refinement may involve the use of live data, adaptive routing logic, and feedback-driven design to more effectively mirror the evolving conditions of urban tourism.
